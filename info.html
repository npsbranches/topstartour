Cities of Uzbekistan:

Tashkent

“Chorsu” market – one of the largest bazaars in Middle Asia, situated in the ancient part of the capital of Uzbekistan Tashkent, called “Eski Shahar”. Chorsu market was well known since Middle Ages and was of big importance on the Great Silk Road.
In the central part of the market there is a monumental done building with the diameter about 300-350 meters - winter 3-storeyed buildings with lifting system. On the lower storey there are cellar corridors with subsidiary rooms. On the middle and higher storey’s there are counters with goods, every visitor can find all necessary goods here.
KHast-Imam complex had been than religious possession nor only off Tashkent but on the whole Uzbekistan for many centuries.
This complex was built with the help of Islamic preacher and the connoisseur of Koran and khadises Khazrati-Imam. His real name was Abu-Bakr Mukhammad Kaffal Shahi.
On the territory of the complex there are also such magnificent objects as Tilla-Sheikh mosque, sacred mosoleum of Kazrati Imam, Islamic institute named after Imam al-Bukhari and madras Barak –Khan.
Rich library of oriental manuscripts is also here. Just here there is that world famous Koran of Khalif Osman consisting of 353 parchment sheets. Just this sacred Koran had traveled through such Arab cities as Medina, Damask, Bagdad and appeared it Uzbekistan by Amir Temur’s initiative.
Minor mosque, situated in Tashkent, attracts hot only it’s religious peculiarity but by it’s beauty and reconciliation. It was built in 2013 before the beginning it the sacred moslem holiday Kurban Hayt.
Magnificent building of white marble stands on the bank of the Ankhor river which makes the mosque and the nearby territory the most attractive place were you can walk and think in silence.
Minor mosque is obviously on the one Centre of Islamic spirituality not only on Tashkent but in Middle Asia.
Amir Temur Square – is the square in the central of Tashkent . In the middle of the square there is a monument to the great military leader Amir Temur, the author of the monument is sculptor Ilkhom Jabfarov. Sitting on the horseback Amir Temur points with his hand the building of Tashkent Juridic University, underliving one of his greatest saings “Strength is in justice”, which is just written on the pedestal of his monument.
Around the square there are such famous buildings at the Hotel “Uzbekistan”, Temurids Museum, well – know Tashkent tower-clock and tremendous Tashkent Forum-palace. Amir Temur Metro station is also here.
Tashkent has always been famous for it’s ancient architect memorials, but owadays it’s fame bring more modern project such as Tashkent Metropoliten. It is a unique system where each station has it’s own peculiarities: original ornament, magnificent elements of lighting.
Three lines are exploiting now, including 29 stations. Besides, the construction of two more lines are being planed now. One of them well be circular.

Khiva

Tash-Khovli Palace – is a building situated on the territory of Icha-Kala of Khiva. It was built by the initiative of Alla Kuli-Khan in the 19th century. It consists of 163 room and 3 yards harem, mekhmonhona (hotel) and arzkhona (court).
The Palace is distinctive by it’s unforgettable palette of paints, pictures and Khiva carving. The facade of building is covered with beautiful and unique ornament.
Juma-mosque may be proudly called the spiritual centre of Khiva. The definite peculiarity of the mosque is that the praying room is the roof which stands on the 200 poles of 4-5 metre high. Each pole is covered with it’s own unique carving.
Islam-Khoja complex is one of the heritages of Icha-Kala. It is famous for it’s minaret, which is the highest in Khiva. All the travellers always found the way to Khiva thanks to it’s magnificent sight.
This complex is called national herigate and perfectly suits ancient landscape of the town. Minaret Kalta-Minor means a short minarets . It had to become the highest minaret in Central Asia, but it’s construction hadn’t been completed. As legend says, the Khan of Khiva found out that his architect had arranged with the ruler of Buhkara to built the highest minaret. After that he threw his architect down from the unfinished minaret But despite it’s unfinished sight it looks very magnificent and unforgetable beauty.
Ichan-Kala – is medieval town in the very heart of Khiva, untouched by the long history. The town is fenced by massive wall 8-10 meters high and 5-6 meters wide and more than 2200 meters long. All these walls make the town very magnificent and forbidding.

Bukhara

Minaret Kalyan – is the sacred symbol of Islamic culture which is situated in Buhkara. This tower has been the highest in Buhkara showing it’s magnificence. The minaret has the name “Kalyan” which means “the great”. At the foot of Minaret Kalyan there are such masterpieces of architecture as Kalyan mosque and madrasah Miri-Arab.
Complex Kaja-Gaukushon is the worship architectural object of Buhkara. The complex consists of madrasah, mosque and minaret, which were built in different years. Minaret a little lower than the minaret Kalyan. Gaukushon means “Killiy bulls” and earlier there was a cattle Killing farme “Lyaby-House” is a square in the centre of Buhkara and famous for it’s beauty.
It is an artificial pool, around which there is a beautiful maarasah and Khanaka Nodira-Divan-Begi. And nearby there is madrasah Kukeldash. Lyabi-Hause means the bank of pool. It is the best place for tourist rest.
Madrasah Miri-Arab can be proudly called a masterpiece of islamic culture. It is situated just opposite Kalyan mosque. In old times madrasah Miri-Arab was the only moslim institution in Central Asia. A great number of them was the former president Chechen Republic Akhmad Kadirov.

Samarkand

Registan square is one of the most magnificent squares not only in Samarkand but the Central Asia. There are three madrasahs in the square – madrasah Ulugbek, madrasah Sherdor and madrasah Tuulya-Kari. Each of these buildings has it’s own unique ornaments the beauty of which calls the feeling of admiration.
The Necropolis Shakhi-Zinda is the only architectoral monument in Samarkand, in which the long-aged history of the city is reflected.
The main building of the complex is the Mosoleum of Kusam-ibn-Abbas. Shakhi-Zinda is famous for it’s elegant and unique beauty of each ornament and decoration.
Gur-Emir is a moloseum where the rules of Temurid’s dynasty are berried. “Gur-Emir ” means – “the tomd of rules” Amir Temur, two his sons – Shohruh and Miranshokh gradsons Mukhamad. Sultan and Mirzo-Ulugbek were berried here. Mosoleum Gur-Emir is the historical herigate of Central Asia, Keepeng a lot of legends and mysteries.
Bibi-Khanum mosque it the biggest in Central Asia famous for it’s beauty and magnificent. The name is translated as “the elder wife”. It got it’s name in honour of the favorite wife of Amir Temur when the military leader returned after his victorius campaign to India.
Near the mosque there is a bazzar which looks very magnificent even after 600 year of it’s existence.
The Plato of demons – is a very mysterious plato situated in Zarafshan highlands not far from Samarkand. It’s origin is just very mysterious and the stones there look like fantastic sculptures which every traveler sees in his different way.
Since 2005 in Samarkand the Theatre of national costumes “Al-Merosi” (People Heritage) has been working. To you attention there is a programme, in which you can get acquainted with the life, habits and traditions of the in habitants of Samarkand, and more important that you can see divers national costumes of different epocs from ancient times till nowadays.
The Palace Sitorai-Mokhi-Khosa is situated not far from Bukhara was the country residence of Bukhara Emirs.
It was built by Muzaffarkhan in honour of his perished young wife Sitora-bonu. The Palce is famous for it unreal beauty and diverse colourful and unique ornaments.
Memorial complex Khodiy Bakhauddin Nakshbandi is the architectoral herigate of Islamic culture. It is situated 12 km from Bukhara. This place is visited by pilgrims from different countries, who come to ask the fulfillment of their willings and the release of sins.

Shakhrisabz

Palace Ak-Saray – is one of the main possessions of Temurids dynast, situated in the town of Shakhrisabz, the native town of Amir Temur. The Palace included many buildings and was surrounded by the wall which made it forbidding fortress.
Complex Dorut-Saodat is a magnificent Building of Shakhrisabz. The name of complex is translated as “the receptacle of authority”.
Firstly the memorial complex was built for berrying of Prince Jakhangir – the son of Amir Temur who died at the age of 20. Some time later the second son of Amir Temur. 29 years old Umarsheikh was berried there.
Kok-Qumbaz is rising above Shakhrisabz a memorial complex with blue dome of 46 metres is diameter. All sounds of voices and actions revive and joining turn into fantastic but at the same time threatening echo. Friday mosque famous for it’s beautiful ornaments is the biggest in the town.
Dorut-Tilovat is the historical monument of Shakhrisabs.
Sheikh Shamsidin Kulal (one of the foundators of sufusim) founded this memorial . He was the mentor of Asim Temur’s father Mukhammad Taragai and Tamerlane. He created the madrasah where they could learn moral and religious habits and the main postulats of Sufism with the area of more than 1500 square km, surrounded by the magnificent mountains and beautiful jumper forests with clearest air. On the banks of the lake there are lots of rest houses, children resorts and many rest zones.

Шахрисабз

Дворец Ак-Сарай - одно из главных достояний династии Темуридов, расположенное в городе ШАХРИСАБЗ, родном городе Амира Темура. Дворец включал в себя множество построек, по периметру был огорожен стеной, что делало его непрестанной крепостью Комплекс Дорус-Саодат - величественное сооружение, расположенное в Шахрисабзе. Ко Название комплекса в переводе на русский означает «вместилище власти». Изначально этот мемориальный комплекс предназначался для захоронения царевича Джахонгира - сына Амира Темура, который скончался в возрасте 20 лет. Спустя некоторое время здесь был похоронен второй сын - двадцатидевятилетний Умаршейх Кок-Гумбаз - возвышающийся над Шахрисабзом мемориальный комплекс с голубым куполом диаметром 46 метров Здесь звуки голосов и действий оживают и, сливаясь, преображаются в причудливое и одновременно грозное эхо. Мечеть, отличающаяся расписными орнаментами и узорами, является самой большой в городе Дорут-Тиловат - исторический памятник Шахрисабзе. Основал это архитектурное сооружение шейх Шамситдин Кулал (один из основоположников суфизма), который был духовным наставником отца Амира Темура Мухаммада Тарагая и самого Тамерлана. Он создал медресе, где можно было изучать нравственные и религиозные обычаи, основы суфизма.

Ташкент

Ташке́нт (узб. Toshkent, Тошкент) — столица и крупнейший город Узбекистана, город республиканского подчинения. Крупнейший по численности населения город Средней Азии — 2 510 800 человек[5], центр Ташкентской городской агломерации, важнейший политический, экономический, культурный и научный центр страны, а также авиационный, железнодорожный и автомобильный узел.

Ташкент — четвёртый город в СНГ[6] по численности населения. Является одним из древнейших городов Средней Азии, в 2009 году отмечалось 2200-летие города[7].

В Ташкенте находятся государственные органы власти, посольства иностранных государств, штаб-квартиры большинства крупнейших узбекистанских коммерческих организаций и общественных объединений.
